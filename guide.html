Build Your Own AI Workout Audio

A step-by-step guide for anyone who wants to use AI and Python to create a personalized, voice-guided workout MP3 — no gym membership, no app subscription.

Start Here: Define Your Goal

Before anything else, be specific about why you're working out. Your goal shapes everything — which exercises to pick, how long to rest, how hard to push.

Example — the routine in this guide: Goal: lower blood pressure and build functional strength.
Approach: isometric and strength training as a complement to running. Low impact, controlled movements, moderate weight. Not about burning calories — about building a body that runs better and stays healthier.

Your goal might be different. Common starting points:

  • Rebuild after injury or a long break
  • Complement cardio (running, cycling) with strength work
  • Lower blood pressure or improve heart health
  • General fitness — feel better day to day
  • Build muscle or lose weight

Step 1 — Generate Your Routine with AI

1 Open any AI assistant (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, etc.) and paste a prompt like this:
"I want to design a home strength training routine. My goal is [your goal]. I have [your equipment]. I'm [beginner / returning after a break / intermediate]. I can work out for about [X] minutes. Give me 8 exercises with: the name, rep count or duration, setup instructions, a go cue for when I start, and a mid-exercise encouragement cue. No ranges — just one clean number for reps or seconds."

Tip: ask the AI to avoid rep ranges like "8–12 reps." Tell it to pick one clean number. Ranges are useless when you're mid-exercise.

2 Review the routine. Ask the AI to swap exercises that don't fit your equipment or ability. Common swaps: chest press → push-ups, jumping jacks → step touches, burpees → anything else.

Step 3 — Generate Your Audio with Python

1 Install the required packages: 
pip install gtts pydub audioop-lts

audioop-lts is required for Python 3.13+. You also need ffmpeg installed on your system: brew install ffmpeg (Mac) or winget install ffmpeg (Windows).

2 Download the Python scripts from the workout-audio GitHub repo. Open generate.py (Workout A) or generate_strength.py (Workout B).
3 At the top of the script, edit the ROUTINE list to match your exercises. Each exercise looks like this: 
{
    "name":     "Wall sit",
    "reps":     "30 seconds",       # what the voice says
    "reps_time": 30,                # how long the audio waits (seconds)
    "setup":    "Back flat against the wall. Slide down until knees are at 90 degrees.",
    "go_cue":   "Hold steady. Easy effort. Keep breathing.",
    "mid_cue":  "Halfway. Breathe. Shoulders relaxed.",
    "timed":    True,               # True = voice counts down the last 5 seconds
},
4 (Optional) Add background music. Create a folder called background music next to the script and drop in any MP3 files. The script shuffles and loops them automatically at a mild volume behind the voice cues. Free sources: Free Music Archive, Pixabay Music.
5 Run the script: 
python generate.py

The output MP3 will appear in the same folder. Transfer it to your phone and play it from the Files or Music app — no internet required mid-workout.

What the Audio Does

  • Announces each exercise with rep count before it starts
  • Gives a setup cue so you know how to get into position
  • Counts down "3, 2, 1 — Go!" with a form reminder
  • Delivers a mid-exercise cue at the halfway point
  • Counts down the final 5 seconds for timed holds
  • Announces the next exercise during the 15-second rest between exercises
  • Handles rest between rounds and the final cool-down cue
